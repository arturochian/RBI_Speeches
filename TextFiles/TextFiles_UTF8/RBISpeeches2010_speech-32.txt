Central Banks must distill Lessons from the Global Crisis and  make Concrete Reforms  Writes Dr. Duvvuri Subbarao, Governor, Reserve Bank of India in an Article in  Finance and Development [F&D] a publication of IMF    Central banks have been at the heart of the global financial crisis. They have  been blamed for policies and actions that got the world into the crisis; they have  been praised for leading the world out of it. Both are fair assessments. Central  banks have been a part of the problem and a part of the solution.  As the crisis unwinds and recovery takes hold, central banks face a number of  issues, of which I will address five.
